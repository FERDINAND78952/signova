Signova: Breaking Communication Barriers

Signova is an innovative hybrid assistive communication system designed to bridge the communication gap for the deaf and hard-of-hearing community in Rwanda and beyond. Leveraging both wearable sensor technology and computer vision, Signova translates Rwandan Sign Language (RSL) gestures into audible speech in real-time, enabling seamless interaction with hearing individuals in everyday scenarios.

Innovative Technology

Wearable sensor gloves and camera-based vision system working together for precise gesture recognition and real-time vocalization.

Social Impact

Empowering users to communicate confidently in social, educational, and professional settings, enhancing inclusion and independence.

Key Features

Real-time translation, specialized for Rwandan Sign Language, hybrid technology, user-friendly interface, and continuous learning.

The Challenge We're Solving

Communication barriers remain a significant challenge for deaf and hard-of-hearing individuals, limiting access to education, employment, and social inclusion. Traditional sign language interpreters are not always available, and existing automated systems often lack localization for regional sign languages such as RSL, reducing their accessibility and effectiveness.

Our Innovative Technology

Signova integrates a dual-input system that combines the best of wearable technology and computer vision:

Wearable Sensor Module

Lightweight gloves embedded with motion sensors (accelerometers, gyroscopes) capture hand orientation, finger positions, and motion dynamics with precision.

Camera-Based Vision System

Using real-time hand gesture recognition through MediaPipe and machine learning models (MobileNetV2 and DenseNet121), the system interprets gestures from visual input.

The system processes these inputs through a trained neural network model to classify gestures accurately. Once recognized, gestures are converted into synthesized voice output using text-to-speech modules, facilitating instant communication with hearing individuals.
